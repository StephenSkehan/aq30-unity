# Forensic Tools — Dark-Grid Contrast Lock

These icons retain the approved Ally Quinn painterly-3D style while being value-optimised for the production dark-blue board.

## Shared correction

Preserve the exact object, tier, silhouette, camera angle, transparent composition, and locked Ally Quinn painterly-3D language. Optimise the icon specifically for the dark-blue board grid. Raise the object's midtone separation by approximately 15%; enlarge and brighten the steel-grey, clinical-cream, teal, and warm-brass material planes that define the outer silhouette; reserve deep navy for internal recesses rather than the entire perimeter. Use a clear upper-left pale key plane, restrained cyan rim only on the cool side, and selective amber/copper rim on the warm side. Do not add an artificial outline, glow, tile, cast shadow, contact shadow, or baked background. Reduce tiny reflections and texture; retain broad sculpted forms and 48 px readability.

## Dusting Brush correction

The Tier 1 brush uses warm clinical-white/ivory bristles with matte black fingerprint powder concentrated across the outer tip. The powder is a single broad dark cap rather than a dust cloud or fine particulate texture.

## Board-readability result

- Tier 1 separates through ivory bristles, black tip, teal grip, and pale steel handle.
- Tier 2 separates through icy plastic planes and the red/cream tamper band.
- Tier 3 separates through the bright teal lid, steel corners, and brass latches.
- Tier 4 separates through the pale lens bezel, violet lens, and brighter blue casing.
- Tier 5 separates through its open silhouette and five internal value accents: ivory brush, cream card, violet lens, pale tubes, and steel tin.

No icon uses a baked outline, glow, shadow, or tile background; contrast is carried entirely by the materials and lighting.
