"Her last message to the show has no words in it. Forty seconds of someone else in her house."

Format canon established by this episode

The tip line: Echoes of Havenbay ends every episode with Ally reading out a voicemail number — "tell me what this city won't." The service is deliberately anonymising: it strips caller identity and keeps only audio and a timestamp, so sources can speak safely. This is why Ally cannot simply call Dot back, why Dot trusted the line mid-intrusion, and how future episodes arrive. Episode temporality: the show publishes case alerts — short rolling updates during an active investigation (L1, L5) — and a full episode after resolution (L12). Podcast-type leads are the recorded show; all other leads are events happening now.

L2 · e1_forty — "The Forty Seconds" (Evidence)

Ally's competence beat. The limp is an observation, not a certainty — confirmed visually at L7.
SpeakerEmotionLine
1AllyneutralYou can't un-hear a message like that. But you can take it apart. Tonight the studio floor belongs to the forty seconds — one sound at a time, until the room gives up its story.
2AllyneutralA floorboard, twice — inside the hall, not the porch. The spring of her back door, stretched slow, the way you open a door you don't want heard. And outside, far off, a diesel. Ticking over. Waiting for somebody.
3AllysurprisedThe steps are the tell. Eight of them, unhurried — and one lands harder than the other, every second step. I can't tell you he limps. I can tell you the house does, when he walks through it.
4AllyneutralSomebody moved through Dot's house at three in the morning, slow and patient, while she kept a line open and her mouth shut. She was braver in forty seconds than most of us manage in a year. Now let's find her street.

L3 · e1_archive — "Three Years of Goodnights" (Data)

The archive as evidence corpus. Establishes Dot's on-air permission (culpability calibration), her dry humour, and the recent trigger.
SpeakerEmotionLine
1AllyneutralThree years of messages, most under a minute. I used to pick one to close the show — Dot knew, and Dot loved it. "Play the one about the gulls," she told me once. "I was very good in that one."
2AllyneutralTonight they're not a closing segment. They're a map. A school bell, always faint, always east. Gulls in the morning messages, never the night ones. And under the late calls, a low mechanical note, every twenty-odd seconds — like the city breathing.
3AllyworriedAnd the boats. She's mentioned them for years the way you'd mention weather. But three weeks ago the message changed: "the pretty one came close tonight — two-note horn, no lights, and a funny mark on the funnel, like a fish-hook." Three weeks ago, I played that one on the show.
4AllyneutralSomewhere in this city is a street with a school bell to the east, gulls at sunrise, and a machine that breathes every twenty seconds. Gerald has heard every machine Havenbay ever built. Time to buy my grandfather a drink.

L6 · e1_cottage — "The Cold Kettle" (Evidence)

The coin, and Gerald's pencil-stop rendered in text. Dot's agency: she packed, she chose.
SpeakerEmotionLine
1AllyneutralEleven Chandler Road. A blue gate, a garden kept the way you keep a promise, mail three days deep — and the spare key exactly where her generation always leaves it. I let myself in. Forgive me, Dot.
2AllysadKettle cold. Bed made. Reading glasses gone, coat gone, the little case she took to church gone. She packed — quick, in the dark, but she packed. You don't pack for the river. She's alive somewhere, and she chose the somewhere.
3AllysurprisedThe hall table: her phone charger, empty — phone gone with her, smart girl. And sitting dead centre on the cradle, where the phone should sleep, a coin. Old brass, worn smooth. Placed. Not dropped.
4AllyworriedI photographed it for Gerald. Watched three dots appear and disappear, twice. Then, no question mark: "Where was it." — "On her phone cradle." A full minute of nothing. Then: "Don't put that on the show, love."
5AllyneutralI have heard my grandfather discuss murders with his mouth full. I have never heard him go quiet. The coin goes in an evidence bag, and the bag goes in the drawer, and the drawer stays shut. Back to work.

L7 · e1_inspector — "The Man Who Came at Noon" (Interview)

The neighbour + doorbell footage. Audio observation → visual confirmation. Intruder's objective concrete; intent stays unknown.
SpeakerEmotionLine
1AllyneutralRivermouth doors open for coffee and a box from the Corner Diner — they pack it heavy when they hear why. Four houses in, a neighbour remembered him: a gas man, ten days back, credentials on a lanyard. No company. Nobody scheduled him.
2AllysurprisedHer doorbell camera kept eleven seconds of him. Tall. Careful. Face angled off-lens like a man who's had practice. And there it is, plain as the audio guessed: every second step lands heavier. The house wasn't imagining. He limps.
3AllyworriedHe went to Dot's door first. Twenty minutes inside, in daylight, with a lanyard for a spell — checking what her window could see, I'd bet my rig on it. Ten days later he came back in the dark, for whatever she'd kept of it.
4AllyneutralWhether he came to frighten her, or silence her, or worse — I don't get to know that yet, and neither do you. What I know is this: he plans, he comes back, and he's on camera. Eleven seconds of him is more than he ever meant anyone to have.

The midpoint inversion. Ally's single angry — the episode's one use, earned.
SpeakerEmotionLine
1AllyneutralBack to the archive, with worse eyes. Every mention of the boats, three years of them, laid end to end. Low in the water going out. Riding high coming back. No lights. No horn — except once.

CHOICE ① Release the forty seconds — let the city hunt with us   ② Hold them back — hunt quiet, keep him blind
5aAllyneutral(path: sets aq.e1.truth_public) We published. The sound of a man being somewhere he shouldn't be, on ten thousand phones by midnight. The queue flooded — one mechanic who knew that engine's tick, and a hundred and ninety-nine ghosts to sift. Loud truth. It costs in noise. It pays in reach.
5bAllyneutral(path: sets aq.e1.truth_protected) We held it. Del's copy went where mine can't — quiet checks on registered diesels, a gait run against old harbour footage. The queue stayed calm, and he stayed blind to what we hold. Quiet truth. It costs in patience. It pays in surprise.
6Allyneutral(converge) Either way, something travels with me now, printed where I'll see it on every case to come: how you tell the truth is also who you are. Dot's still out there. Next question — how does a frightened woman with a church case leave town without her car?
