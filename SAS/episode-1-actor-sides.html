Ally Quinn: True Crime Merge · Echoes of Havenbay

Episode 1 — The Listener · Actor Sides

VO LOCK v1.0 · locked 11 July 2026 · these lines are final — report any stumble to production; do not improvise fixes
Context for both actors: Echoes of Havenbay is a true-crime podcast. Grounded, intimate, noir-lite — never campy, never announcer-voiced. The microphone is close. Ally speaks to one listener at a time. Dot is a 78-year-old widow leaving voicemails to a show she loves: warm, dry-witted, unhurried, never feeble.

ALLY QUINN — 13 cues

E1-03 · The opening
polished host voice, fatigue underneath
My show ends the same way every week: I read out the tip-line number and say, tell me what this city won't. The line keeps voices, timestamps and nothing else. That's the promise. For three years, Dot left the last message after every episode.
E1-04
remorse under restraint — the guilt is real, the control is professional
Three nights ago — 3:04 on Friday morning — she left the message you just heard a piece of. Forty seconds. No words. She opened the line and let the house speak for her. It waited three days in my queue. I was busy making a show about listening.
E1-05
resolve; returning to host register
Dot, if you can hear this: goodnight, the harbour misses you. Everyone else — this is Echoes of Havenbay. The tip line is open. This week, it's mine to answer.
E1-06 · The case alert
host register, steadier than she feels
This is Echoes of Havenbay, with a case alert. Her name is Dot Ellis, from Rivermouth. At 3:04 on Friday morning, someone was inside her house. She hasn't been heard from since.
E1-07
level, factual
Police logged a welfare check. No forced entry, no report from family, and an adult is allowed to leave home without explaining herself. They won't call it an abduction. I'm not asking them to — yet.
E1-08
direct appeal
So we do what this show does. We knock on Rivermouth's doors ourselves — and you'll notice I'm not saying her street on this feed. If you knew Dot Ellis, the tip line is open, and it keeps no names.
E1-09
quiet steel; speaking to one particular listener
One more thing, for whoever needs to hear it. She left us your footsteps. Eight of them — eight steps too slow to stay invisible. That was Friday. We're faster now.
E1-10 · The closing episode
host register, warmer than the opening
This is Echoes of Havenbay. Dot Ellis is safe. She was found through the life she left in plain sight — a school, a parish, a bus route, and three years of messages nobody had thought to treat as evidence. She declined an interview. Her words: "I'm not content, love. I'm a person."
E1-11 · Branch A — record both variants
owning the cost of the loud road
On the record: the van traces to Harbourline Security — dissolved in 2011, still driving. You heard the full track when we did, and it worked, and it cost: two hundred ghosts in the queue, and the van gone before police reached it. Loud truth. I'd choose it again. I think.
E1-12 · Branch B — record both variants
owning the cost of the quiet road
On the record: the van traces to a dissolved port-security company. Del has asked me to keep its name off this feed — and this time, I'm listening. Quiet truth cost an afternoon of silence I don't want to spend again. I'd choose it again. I think.
E1-13
plain; a rule being made, not a speech
And one thing changes at this show, starting tonight: a voice can tell us where to look without me telling everyone else where it lives. That's the new rule. Dot wrote it; I'm just reading it out.
E1-14
intimate and controlled — not a costume change
And now — not as a reporter, but to the man with the careful walk: this show is built on listening. This case taught me who else does. She's safe. The evidence is safe. You're the only one still hiding.
E1-16 · The last line
four words, then the promise. Let the pause before the promise do the work
Goodnight, Dot. Goodnight, harbour. — The tip line stays open.
