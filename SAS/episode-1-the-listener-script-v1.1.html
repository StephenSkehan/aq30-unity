"Her last message to the show has no words in it. Forty seconds of someone else in her house."

Format canon

The tip line: "The tip line keeps voices, timestamps and nothing else. That's the promise." Deliberately anonymising — sources can speak safely; nobody, including Ally, can call a caller back. Episode temporality: the show publishes short case alerts during an active investigation (L1, L5) and the full episode after resolution (L12). Podcast leads are the recorded show; everything else happens now. In-world clock: datelines per lead below; the final message is three nights old when the episode opens and stays "three nights" at every mention.

Cold open. Dot first. Node 2 plays an 8–12 second EXCERPT only — the full forty seconds is L2's interactive object. Caption is deliberately non-specific: accessibility gives equivalent information, not the solution.

L2 · e1_forty — "The Forty Seconds" (Evidence) · Day 1

The full recording as the player's object. Precise sound labels unlock HERE, not in the cold open. Floorboard located by evidence (older messages), not intuition. Competence beat.
SpeakerEmotionLine
1AllyneutralI loaded the whole message into the studio. Forty seconds, one sound at a time.
2AllyneutralFirst: a floorboard, twice. The same squeak turns up in twelve of her older messages — always three steps after she answers, coming from the hall. Whoever moved last Thursday was standing where Dot stands to talk to me.
3AllyneutralThen a strained spring — her back door, opened slow. The way you open a door you don't want heard. And outside, faint but steady: a diesel engine, idling. Waiting for somebody.
4AllysurprisedThe steps are the tell. Eight of them, unhurried, and every second one lands harder. I can't tell you he limps. I can tell you the house does, when he walks through it.
5AllyneutralSomebody was in Dot's house at three in the morning. She kept the line open and her mouth shut — braver in forty seconds than most of us manage in a year. Now let's find her street.

L3 · e1_archive — "Three Years of Goodnights" (Data) · Day 1–2

Archive as evidence corpus. Permission established. Bell identified by schedule, not compass. Trigger planted.
SpeakerEmotionLine
1AllyneutralThree years of messages, most under a minute. I used to pick one to close the show — Dot knew, and loved it. "Play the one about the gulls," she told me once. "I was very good in that one."
2AllyneutralTonight they're not a closing segment. They're a map. A school bell in the daytime messages — half past eight, then noon, term time only. And under the late calls, a low mechanical note every twenty-odd seconds.
3AllyworriedAnd the boats. Years of them, mentioned like weather. Then three weeks ago the message changed: "the pretty one came close tonight — two-note horn, no lights, and a funny mark on the funnel, like a fish-hook." I played that one on the show.
4AllyneutralA school that rings at eight-thirty and noon. A machine that breathes every twenty seconds. Gerald has heard every machine this city ever built. Time to buy my grandfather a drink.

L4 · e1_bridge — "The Volume-Up" (Discuss — Gerald, the Rusty Anchor) · Day 2

Gerald's entrance, process shown. Mo earns her identification from the audio itself.
SpeakerEmotionLine
1AllyneutralThe Anchor, Tuesday-quiet, the day after I pulled the archive apart. Mo had the corner booth held before I asked. I handed Gerald the headphones and told him what was on them. He didn't flinch. He turned the volume up.
2GeraldneutralLoop the low note for me, love. Just that. Nothing else in my ears.
3AllyneutralI looped it. He counted under his breath, pencil ticking the table. Then he took the headphones off. Twenty-two seconds.
4GeraldworriedSwing-bridge counterweight — Rivermouth reach. The old crossing sings when boats run late. Your Dot lives close enough to hear the river breathe. So did the man in her hall. Mind how you go down there.
5AllyneutralOn the way out I played Mo three seconds of Dot's voice. She stopped polishing the glass. "Dorothy Ellis. Cleaned Chandler Road school thirty years. Tuesday quiz, table six — tell her it misses her." Rivermouth. Chandler Road. We're close.

L5 · e1_pod1 — "Case Alert: Dot Ellis" (Podcast, VO — milestone) · Day 2

Welfare-check reframe — police neither absent nor absurd. The threat line claims only what the evidence supports. Fires e1.phase1.complete.
SpeakerEmotionLine
1Allyneutral[host register, steadier than she feels] This is Echoes of Havenbay, with a case alert. Her name is Dot Ellis, of Chandler Road, Rivermouth. Three nights ago someone was inside her house at three in the morning. She hasn't been heard from since.
2AllyneutralPolice logged a welfare check. No forced entry, no report from family, and an adult is allowed to leave home without explaining herself. They won't call it an abduction. I'm not asking them to — yet.
3AllyworriedSo we do what this show does. We knock on Rivermouth's doors ourselves. If you knew Dot Ellis, the tip line is open — and it keeps no names.
4Allyneutral[quiet steel; to one listener] One more thing, for whoever needs to hear it. She left us your footsteps. Eight of them — eight steps too slow to stay invisible. That was three nights ago. We're faster now.

L6 · e1_cottage — "The Cold Kettle" (Evidence) · Day 3

Sanctioned entry via Mrs. Vale. Car established. Evidence handled properly — glass over the coin, Del collects. Gerald's warning intact.
SpeakerEmotionLine
1AllyneutralEleven Chandler Road. A blue gate, a garden kept the way you keep a promise — and Dot's car in the drive, dry ground beneath it. It hasn't moved since the rain.
2AllyneutralMrs. Vale next door has held the spare key for years — Dot's own arrangement: use it if the curtains stay shut two mornings running. This was the third. She went in first. I followed.
3AllysadKettle cold. Bed made. Reading glasses gone, coat gone, the little case she took to church gone. She packed — quick, in the dark, but she packed. You don't pack for the river.
4AllysurprisedThe hall table: her phone charger, empty. The phone was gone. I hoped that meant it went with her. And on the cradle, dead centre, where the phone should sleep — a coin. Old brass, worn smooth. Placed. Not dropped.
5AllyworriedI didn't touch it. I put a drinking glass over it, photographed where it lay, and sent the picture to two people. Del said she'd collect it properly. Gerald's answer took a full minute to arrive: "Where was it." Then: "Don't put that on the show, love."
6AllyneutralI've heard my grandfather discuss murders with his mouth full. I've never heard him go quiet. Back to work.

L7 · e1_inspector — "The Man Who Came at Noon" (Interview) · Day 3

Mrs. Vale named and useful. Limp confirmed visually. Objective framed as a working theory.
SpeakerEmotionLine
1AllyneutralRivermouth doors open for coffee and a box from the Corner Diner — they packed it heavy when they heard why. Mrs. Vale remembered him without prompting: a gas man, ten days back, lanyard and clipboard. No company name she knew. Nobody had scheduled anything.
2AllysurprisedHer doorbell camera kept eleven seconds of him. Tall. Careful. Face angled off-lens like a man with practice. And there it is, plain as the audio guessed — every second step lands heavier. The house wasn't imagining. He limps.
3AllyworriedHe went to Dot's door first. Twenty minutes inside, in daylight, with a lanyard for a spell. My working theory: the first visit mapped what her window could see. The second came back for whatever she'd kept of it.
4AllyneutralWhether he came to frighten her, or silence her, or worse — I don't get to know that yet, and neither do you. What I know: he plans, he returns, and he's on camera. Eleven seconds is more than he ever meant anyone to have.

L8 · e1_boats — "The Quiet Boats" (Money Trail) · Day 3

The Money Trail label earned: cargo one way, money the other. Midpoint inversion framed as supported inference. Ally's single angry.
SpeakerEmotionLine
1AllyneutralBack to the archive with worse eyes. Every boat mention, three years, laid end to end. Low in the water going out. Riding high coming back. No lights, no horn. Something heavy leaves this city at night — and whatever comes back is lighter and worth more.
2AllyneutralInsomnia made Dot a scientist. Same window, same hours, decades of nights. She wasn't spying. She just knew what belonged on her river — and said hello to what didn't.
3AllysurprisedThree weeks ago: the pretty one, close in. Two-note horn. A fish-hook mark on the funnel. I played it on the show with her blessing and thought it was charming.
4Allyangry[cold precision, not volume] Ten days later a fake inspector mapped her window. If he found her through the show — then I handed him the window. Episode forty-one, closing minute, my voice, her view. And if he's a listener, he's heard everything I've said since.

L9 · e1_delcruz — "Off the Record" (Interview — THE BRANCH) · Day 4

Del Cruz: direct dialogue, portrait (canon set). Balanced branch: public = faster, noisier; protected = slower, cleaner. Path nodes set the truth flag.

4Del CruzneutralYour show, your call. Just don't pretend silence is neutral either.
CHOICE ① Release the forty seconds — the city hunts with us   ② Hold them back — hunt quiet, keep him blind
5aAllyneutral(sets aq.e1.truth_public) We published that night. By morning, a retired Harbourline fleet mechanic had named the engine — a governor whine their vans all carried after a recall. Hours, not days. The cost came with it: two hundred false sightings, and a van that vanished off the streets by noon.
5bAllyneutral(sets aq.e1.truth_protected) We held it. Del walked the footage through properly: the van's plate traces to a shell contractor, but its VIN was last registered to Harbourline. A day slower than a crowd would have been — and he still doesn't know what we hold. Clean costs patience. I paid it in silence.
6Allyneutral(converge) Either way, one thing rides with me now: how you tell the truth is also who you are. Dot's still out there — and her car never left the drive. So how does a frightened woman with a church case leave town?

SpeakerEmotionLine
1AllyneutralThe cottage told us she left. Now, where. A pharmacy bag folded flat in the kitchen drawer — St. Brigid's Pharmacy, Larkhill. A town I had to look up.
2AllyneutralInside the parish newsletter on her mantel, a Christmas card in someone else's hand. "The hills are quiet. You'd hate it. — V." No address. No surname. The first evidence Dot has family anywhere.

4AllyneutralVera. That's my guess for "V" — mentioned twice in three years, once at Christmas, once like an apology. I'm driving up at first light. If I'm wrong, it's a long quiet road to be wrong on.

L11 · e1_hills — "The Hill Cottage" (Location — CLIMAX) · Day 5

Organic tension: the door opens because Dot heard the gate. The diesel pays a partial confirmation. Ally apologises — properly. Dot's angry is the script's second and last.
SpeakerEmotionLine
1AllyworriedFog all the way up — the kind that swallows headlights whole. I found the church, then the street. And before I saw anything at all, I heard it: a diesel, idling, somewhere behind the white.
2AllyworriedThrough the fog, for two seconds, it moved: a pale van, pulling away without lights. One rear lamp dead. The same dead lamp as eleven seconds of doorbell footage. I stood in the road and let it go, because down the lane a gate was in reach and a van wasn't.
3AllyworriedThe cottage door opened before I reached the step — a shape behind the lace curtain, small and absolutely unbothered.
4DotneutralYou always did make a production of things. I heard the gate. Vera heard the gate. The church heard the gate. Come in, love — you're letting the hill in.
5DotangryAnd before you say sorry, say it properly: you turned my window into a broadcast, Ally Quinn. My river. My own silly voice. — And I still called your line that night. You know why? I knew you'd hear it. Eventually.
6AllysadI said I was sorry. Properly. No microphone, no defence, while Vera pretended the kettle needed watching. Then I said goodnight the way Dot taught me, and told her about the van. She's safe tonight. "Tonight" is doing a lot of work in that sentence.
