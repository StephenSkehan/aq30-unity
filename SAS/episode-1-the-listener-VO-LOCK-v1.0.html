3Allyneutral[polished host voice, fatigue underneath] My show ends the same way every week: I read out the tip-line number and say, tell me what this city won't. The line keeps voices, timestamps and nothing else. That's the promise. For three years, Dot left the last message after every episode.
▶ FIRST MERGE GOAL — "Set up the rig." Deterministic single merge. Nodes 4–5 on completion.
4Allysad[remorse under restraint] Three nights ago — 3:04 on Friday morning — she left the message you just heard a piece of. Forty seconds. No words. She opened the line and let the house speak for her. It waited three days in my queue. I was busy making a show about listening.
5Allyneutral[resolve; host register] Dot, if you can hear this: goodnight, the harbour misses you. Everyone else — this is Echoes of Havenbay. The tip line is open. This week, it's mine to answer.

L2 · e1_forty — "The Forty Seconds" (Evidence) · Day 1

Ungendered intruder; unidentified door — evidential language until L7/L11 verify.
SpeakerEmotionLine
1AllyneutralI loaded the whole of Friday's message into the studio. Forty seconds, one sound at a time.
2AllyneutralFirst: a floorboard, twice. The same squeak opens twelve of her older messages — the same three footsteps before she starts talking. Her hall, by the phone table. Whoever moved on Friday was standing where Dot stands to talk to me.
3AllyneutralThen a strained spring — a door, opened slow. The way you open one you don't want heard. And outside, faint but steady: a diesel engine, idling. Waiting for somebody.
4AllysurprisedThe steps are the tell. Eight of them, unhurried, and every second one lands harder. I can't call it a limp yet. I can tell you the house does when they cross it.
5AllyneutralSomebody was in Dot's house at three in the morning. She kept the line open and her mouth shut — braver in forty seconds than most of us manage in a year. Now let's find her street.

L3 · e1_archive — "Three Years of Goodnights" (Data) · Day 1, Monday evening

SpeakerEmotionLine
1AllyneutralThree years of messages, most under a minute. I used to pick one to close the show — Dot knew, and loved it. "Play the one about the gulls," she told me once. "I was very good in that one."
2AllyneutralTonight they're not a closing segment. They're a map. A school bell in the daytime messages — half past eight, then noon, term time only. And under the late calls, a low mechanical note every twenty-odd seconds.
3AllyworriedAnd the boats. Years of them, mentioned like weather. Then three weeks ago the message changed: "the pretty one came close tonight — two-note horn, no lights, and a funny mark on the funnel, like a fish-hook." I played that one on the show.
4AllyneutralA school that rings at eight-thirty and noon. A machine that breathes every twenty seconds. Gerald has heard every machine this city ever built. Time to buy my grandfather a drink.

L4 · e1_bridge — "The Volume-Up" (Discuss — Gerald) · Day 2, Tuesday

SpeakerEmotionLine
1AllyneutralThe Anchor, Tuesday-quiet. Mo had the corner booth held before I asked. I handed Gerald the headphones and told him what was on them. He didn't flinch. He turned the volume up.
2GeraldneutralLoop the low note for me, love. Just that. Nothing else in my ears.
3AllyneutralI looped it. He counted under his breath, pencil ticking the table. Then he took the headphones off. Twenty-two seconds.
4GeraldworriedSwing-bridge counterweight — Rivermouth reach. The old crossing sings when boats run late. Your Dot lives close enough to hear the river breathe. So did whoever was in her hall. Mind how you go down there.
5AllyneutralOn the way out I played Mo three seconds of Dot's voice. She stopped polishing the glass. "Dorothy Ellis. Cleaned Chandler Road school for thirty years. Tuesday quiz, table six — tell her it misses her." Rivermouth. Chandler Road. We're close.

L5 · e1_pod1 — "Case Alert: Dot Ellis" (Podcast, VO — milestone) · Day 2

SpeakerEmotionLine
1Allyneutral[host register, steadier than she feels] This is Echoes of Havenbay, with a case alert. Her name is Dot Ellis, from Rivermouth. At 3:04 on Friday morning, someone was inside her house. She hasn't been heard from since.
2AllyneutralPolice logged a welfare check. No forced entry, no report from family, and an adult is allowed to leave home without explaining herself. They won't call it an abduction. I'm not asking them to — yet.
3AllyworriedSo we do what this show does. We knock on Rivermouth's doors ourselves — and you'll notice I'm not saying her street on this feed. If you knew Dot Ellis, the tip line is open, and it keeps no names.
4Allyneutral[quiet steel; to one listener] One more thing, for whoever needs to hear it. She left us your footsteps. Eight of them — eight steps too slow to stay invisible. That was Friday. We're faster now.

L6 · e1_cottage — "The Cold Kettle" (Evidence) · Day 3, Wednesday

Mrs. Vale's arrangement without arithmetic. Toasts: "Mail: days deep" (~30%) · "The kitchen: packed in the dark" (~70%).
SpeakerEmotionLine
1AllyneutralEleven Chandler Road. A blue gate, a garden kept the way you keep a promise — and Dot's car in the drive, dry ground beneath it. It hasn't moved since before the rain.
2AllyneutralMrs. Vale next door has held the spare key for years — Dot's own arrangement: if the curtains stayed shut and she couldn't raise her, use it. She couldn’t — and hadn’t for days. She went in first. I followed.
3AllysadKettle cold. Bed made. Reading glasses gone, coat gone, the little case she took to church gone. She packed — quick, in the dark, but she packed. You don't pack for the river.
4AllysurprisedThe hall table: her phone charger, empty. The phone was gone. I hoped that meant it went with her. And on the cradle, dead centre, where the phone should sleep — a coin. Old brass, worn smooth. Placed. Not dropped.
5AllyworriedI didn't touch it. I photographed where it lay, backed us both out of the hall, and kept the room untouched until Del arrived. Gerald's answer to the photo took a full minute: "Where was it." Then: "Don't put that on the show, love."
6AllyneutralI've heard my grandfather discuss murders with his mouth full. I've never heard him go quiet. Back to work.

L7 · e1_inspector — "The Man Who Came at Noon" (Interview) · Day 3

Gender established here. Dead lamp AND readable plate planted — grounds L9's trace and L11's payoff.
SpeakerEmotionLine
1AllyneutralRivermouth doors open for coffee and a box from the Corner Diner — they packed it heavy when they heard why. Mrs. Vale remembered him without prompting: a gas man, eight days before Friday’s message, lanyard doing the work. No company name she knew. Nobody had scheduled anything.
2AllysurprisedHer doorbell camera kept eleven seconds of him. Tall. Careful. Face angled off-lens like a man with practice. Every second step lands heavier — the house wasn't imagining. At the kerb, a pale van, left rear lamp dead. And as it pulled away, one clean frame of the rear plate.
3AllyworriedHe went to Dot's door first. Twenty minutes inside, in daylight. My working theory: the first visit mapped what her window could see. The second came back for whatever she'd kept of it.
4AllyneutralWhether he came to frighten her, or silence her, or worse — I don't get to know that yet, and neither do you. What I know: he plans, he returns, and he's on camera. Eleven seconds, a dead lamp and one clean plate are more than he ever meant anyone to have.

L8 · e1_boats — "The Quiet Boats" (Money Trail) · Day 3

Money Trail now literal: cash mooring fees. Toasts: "Pattern: low out, high back" (~30%) · "Three weeks ago: the fish-hook" (~70%). Ally's single angry.
SpeakerEmotionLine
1AllyneutralBack to the archive with worse eyes. Every boat mention, three years, laid end to end. Low in the water going out. Riding high coming back. No manifest ever filed for a night crossing on that reach — but the mooring fees are paid, in cash, quarterly, by a company with no phone number. Cargo that doesn't exist, paid for by money that does.
2AllyneutralInsomnia made Dot a scientist. Same window, same hours, decades of nights. She wasn't spying. She just knew what belonged on her river — and said hello to what didn't.
3AllysurprisedThree weeks ago: the pretty one, close in. Two-note horn. A fish-hook mark on the funnel. I played it on the show with her blessing and thought it was charming.
4Allyangry[cold precision, not volume] Ten days after I aired the fish-hook message, a fake inspector mapped her window. If he found her through the show — then I handed him the window. Episode forty-one, closing minute, my voice, her view. And if he's a listener, he's heard everything I've said since.

L9 · e1_delcruz — "Off the Record" (Interview — THE BRANCH) · Day 4, Thursday

SpeakerEmotionLine
1AllyneutralSergeant Del Cruz, Harbor Ward, her lunch break, her bench — back to the water. I laid it out: the forty seconds, the inspector, the van, the coin now in her evidence intake.
2Del CruzneutralI can open the missing-person file — empty house, packed bag, that footage. That's real. What I can't do is turn your theory about boats into warrants. Not on a podcast's say-so.
3AllyworriedThen the question that cuts this episode in half. He already knows I heard him — I told the whole city I have his footsteps. What he doesn't know is how cleanly. The full track holds his engine, his gait, the room itself. Publish it, and ten thousand ears go to work. So does he.
4Del CruzneutralYour show, your call. Just don't pretend silence is neutral either.
CHOICE ① Release the full recording — let the city work it   ② Publish a transcript only — Del keeps the clean copy
5aAllyneutral(sets aq.e1.truth_public) We released it at noon. By mid-afternoon, a retired Harbourline fleet mechanic had named the engine — the governor retrofit their vans carried after the '09 recall. Hours, not days. The cost came with it: two hundred false sightings, and the van off the streets by dusk.
5bAllyneutral(sets aq.e1.truth_protected) We published the transcript and held the audio. By evening Del had walked the clean copy and the plate through properly: the plate is registered to a shell contractor, but the VIN traces back to a van last registered to Harbourline. Slower than the crowd. Cleaner than the noise. And he still doesn't know what we hold.
6Allyneutral(converge) Either way, one thing rides with me now: how you tell the truth is also who you are. Dot's still out there — and her car never left the drive. So how does a frightened woman with a church case leave town?

L10 · e1_trail — "Where Dot Went" (Data) · Day 4, evening

SpeakerEmotionLine
1AllyneutralThe cottage told us she left. Now, where. A pharmacy bag folded flat in the kitchen drawer — St. Brigid's Pharmacy, Larkhill. A town I had to look up.
2AllyneutralTucked inside the parish newsletter on her mantel, a Christmas card in someone else's hand. "The hills are quiet. You'd hate it. — V." The first evidence that Vera was more than a name Dot mentioned when she was feeling guilty.
3AllyneutralAnd folded into the same newsletter, a bus timetable: one morning service from Rivermouth passes through Larkhill. Pharmacy, card, route. Not proof. Enough to ring the depot.
4AllyneutralThe morning driver remembered her — the only passenger to ask for Chapel Lane before sunrise, church case on her lap. Chapel Lane, Larkhill. I'm driving up at first light. If I'm wrong, it's a long quiet road to be wrong on.

L11 · e1_hills — "The Hill Cottage" (Location — CLIMAX) · Day 5, Friday

Toasts: "The fog thickens past the church" (~30%) · "A diesel, somewhere ahead" (~70%) → completion launches the van sighting. Beta telemetry watch on this lead.
SpeakerEmotionLine
1AllyworriedFog all the way up — the kind that swallows headlights whole. I found the church, then Chapel Lane. And before I saw anything at all, I heard it: a diesel, idling, somewhere behind the white.
2AllyworriedThrough the fog, for two seconds, it moved: a pale van pulling away without lights. One rear lamp dead — the same dead lamp caught in Mrs. Vale's eleven seconds of footage. I stood in the road and let it go. A gate was in reach and a van wasn't.
3AllyworriedThe cottage door opened before I reached the step — a shape behind the lace curtain, small and absolutely unbothered.
4DotneutralYou always did make a production of things. I heard the gate. Vera heard the gate. The church heard the gate. Come in, love — you're letting the hill in.
5DotangryAnd before you say sorry, say it properly: you turned my window into a broadcast, Ally Quinn. My river. My own silly voice. — And I still called your line that night. You know why? I knew you'd hear it. Eventually.
6DotneutralI went out through the allotments and caught the first bus. Phone switched off since — the fake gas man handled it at noon, and I wasn't leading him to Vera. Your number was the one I could dial in the dark. I needed the sound somewhere he couldn't take it.
7AllysadI said I was sorry. Properly. No microphone, no defence, while Vera pretended the kettle needed watching. Then I told them about the van.
8AllyneutralDel had a unit on Chapel Lane before that kettle boiled, and Dot and Vera spent the night somewhere the van doesn't know. She's safe tonight. "Tonight" is doing a lot of work in that sentence.

SpeakerEmotionLine
1Allyneutral[host register, warmer than the episode's opening] This is Echoes of Havenbay. Dot Ellis is safe. Her words: "I'm not content, love. I'm a person."
2aAllyworried(requires aq.e1.truth_public) On the record: the van traces to Harbourline Security — dissolved in 2011, still driving. 2bAllyworried(requires aq.e1.truth_protected) On the record: the van traces to a dissolved port-security company. Del has asked me to keep its name off this feed — and this time, I'm listening. Quiet truth cost an afternoon of silence I don't want to spend again. I'd choose it again. I think.
3AllyneutralAnd one thing changes at this show, starting tonight: a voice can tell us where to look without me telling everyone else where it lives. That's the new rule. Dot wrote it; I'm just reading it out.
4Allyworried[intimate and controlled — not a costume change] And now — not as a reporter, but to the man with the careful walk: this show is built on listening. This case taught me who else does. She's safe. The evidence is safe. You're the only one still hiding.
5Dot (voicemail)happy[warm; the ritual, reclaimed] It's Dot. Not from Rivermouth tonight — imagine that. Vera says hello, which for Vera is a parade. You found an old woman who didn't want a fuss and you made one anyway. Good girl. Say goodnight to the harbour for me, Ally.
6Allyneutral[four words, then the promise] Goodnight, Dot. Goodnight, harbour. — The tip line stays open.
7——[Somewhere: this episode, playing. A click. Silence. A diesel engine starts.]
