## How to use

1. Attach **Ally model sheet 4A (neutral-light identity reference)** as the style reference with every generation — it supersedes the app icon as anchor (2026-07-11). Say "match this art style" — never "match this character". Blue-is-lighting rule applies: blue tones in any noir variant are lighting only, never material color.
2. Paste the **Global Style Block**, then one **Character Block**.
3. Generate the **neutral** portrait first. Approve it. Then generate emotions using the Variant Template with the approved neutral attached as the character reference.
4. Order of production (by first likely story use): Mo → Del Cruz → Arthur → Helen → Priya → Cortez → Vega.

```
Match the attached image's art style exactly: stylized painterly 3D-illustration
look, expressive oversized eyes, soft cinematic key light with a warm rim light,
rich saturated color against muted noir backgrounds. This is a character portrait
for a true-crime detective game set in a coastal city at night.

Format: bust portrait (head and shoulders to mid-chest), three-quarter view
facing slightly toward the viewer's left, eyes toward camera. Square 1024x1024.
Background: plain very dark navy (#0A1220), softly vignetted, no scenery, no
text, no watermark. Neutral resting expression with personality — not blank.
One character only, centered, head fully in frame with margin above the hair.
```

## Emotion Strip Template (rev 2026-07-11 — supersedes single-variant template)

One generation per character produces the full 7-emotion sheet. Attach the
character's ★ CANON approved portrait as the identity reference.

```
Using the attached image as the exact character reference: produce a MODEL SHEET
of this same character showing SEVEN emotion portraits arranged in two rows
(4 on top, 3 below), each labeled beneath in small grey type:
1 NEUTRAL, 2 HAPPY, 3 SAD, 4 ANGRY, 5 SURPRISED, 6 WORRIED, 7 CONFUSED.

Every cell: identical face, hair, clothing, accessories, lighting and camera
angle to the attached reference — bust crop, head and shoulders only, HANDS OUT
OF FRAME. Same head size in every cell, evenly spaced, plain background matching
the reference. Only the facial expression changes between cells.

The seven expressions must be distinguishable from each other at thumbnail size:
- NEUTRAL: the reference expression, at rest.
- HAPPY: genuine smile reaching the eyes.
- SAD: heavy-lidded, gaze dropped, mouth corners down — grief, drooping DOWNWARD.
- ANGRY: brows driven down and together, jaw set, mouth hard — unmistakable at a glance.
- SURPRISED: eyes wide, brows high, mouth slightly open.
- WORRIED: brows pulled TOGETHER and UP in the middle, eyes wide and alert, lips
  pressed — tension and apprehension, clearly different from SAD (worried looks
  UP and OUT at a threat; sad looks DOWN and IN).
- CONFUSED: head tilted a few degrees, ONE eyebrow raised, slight asymmetric
  frown — clearly different from NEUTRAL.
No text other than the seven labels. No watermark.
```

Per-character appends (paste after the block):
- **Ally**: `Keep the headphones around the neck and the silver locket in every cell.`
- **Dot**: `Keep the glasses chain and gold scallop brooch in every cell. Her ANGRY
  is COLD anger — jaw set, eyes hard, smile gone; dignified, never shouting. Her
  HAPPY is a dry, warm crinkle — amused, not beaming.`
- **Del**: `Keep the badge chain and Saint Michael medallion in every cell. Her
  HAPPY is a restrained professional half-smile, warmth mostly in the eyes. Her
  ANGRY is controlled command presence, not rage.`

Acceptance per sheet: identity consistent across all 7 · accessories present in
all 7 · sad/worried clearly different · confused ≠ neutral · labels in engine
order. If one cell fails, regenerate the whole sheet (cheaper than matching a
single cell later).

Import: I slice the strip into the 7 per-emotion PNGs — deliver the sheet at the
highest resolution available.
