# Market Research: What Makes Narrative-Merge Games Win — and Where AQ30 Stands
*Research date: 2026-07-10 · sources linked at end*

## Part 1 — What the evidence says

### The genre is the right bet
Merge revenue has roughly **quadrupled since 2022**, and the specific growth story is *narrative-driven* merge: Gossip Harbor cleared **$115M+ in monthly IAP** by December 2025 with ~15–20% more from ads, and its publisher's portfolio now competes with King and Playrix. Analysts explicitly attribute its rise to "growing interest in narrative-driven merge games." AQ30's genre choice is validated by the market's biggest recent success — we are not swimming against the current.

### The success factors, ranked by evidence strength

1. **Story as the core motivation, not decoration.** Gossip Harbor's deconstructions consistently credit its emotional, character-driven serial drama (a protagonist rebuilding her life; secrets; a central mystery) presented like a visual novel and *interleaved with* play, not gated behind it. The player motivation it captures — "role-playing & emotions" — pulls in audiences beyond the puzzle core. **This is the factor AQ30 is now explicitly built around**, and yesterday's Episode 1 replacement decision is precisely this lesson applied.

2. **A meta-layer beyond the board.** Nearly every top-grossing merge game adds a persistent progress space — renovation, decoration, collection — that converts merge output into visible, ownable change. Analysts call a decoration meta "essential to stand out." *AQ30's evidence board is a proto-meta* (a space that fills and visibly changes as you progress) but it is currently passive.

3. **Hybrid monetization with heavy rewarded-video appetite.** The winning revenue split in casual merge trends toward ~45/55 IAP/ads, and merge players watch a *lot* of rewarded video — genre averages around 100 videos per user. Top-performer IAP ARPDAU: Gossip Harbor at ~$0.31, roughly double competitors; puzzle genre baseline nearer $0.08.

4. **LiveOps and events as the revenue engine.** Event adoption is rising across every event type — collectible albums, win-streaks, co-op missions, holiday bundles. Events create "consistent daily purchase moments that lift ARPDAU without hurting retention." Gossip Harbor's holiday event bundles measurably spiked revenue.

5. **Session design: short satisfying bursts + a long arc.** The winning cadence is 5–10 minute sessions that each complete a beat, inside a serial story that never completes. Energy systems enforce the cadence; story cliffs make the return valuable.

6. **Benchmarks to hold ourselves to:** casual merge D1 retention ≈ **25%**, D7 ≈ **3–7%**. These are the numbers the Sprint 9 beta funnel should be read against.

### Distribution reality for a solo studio
- **50–70% of installs come from organic store search** → ASO (keywords, screenshots, title) is not a checkbox, it is the primary acquisition channel.
- Paid UA auctions now favor deep-pocketed buyers; indie marketing that works is **short-form video** (TikTok/Shorts/Reels) where one resonant clip does the work of a UA budget, plus community building.
- Episodic narrative indies succeed on "satisfying beats in 5–10 minute bursts" — matching our lead structure.

## Part 3 — Honest bottom line

AQ30 v1 is a **quality-first niche entry, not a Gossip Harbor competitor at launch** — and that's the correct strategy for a solo studio with no UA budget. We cannot out-liveops Microfun; we *can* out-write everyone in the subgenre, which the evidence says is the motivation that recruits players from outside the puzzle core. Launch should be read as **validation and audience seeding**: measure D1 against the ~25% genre benchmark, expect modest ARPDAU with one ad placement and no events, and treat reviews/story-completion rate as the leading indicators that the differentiator works.

The evidence-backed gaps are all **post-launch roadmap items, in this order**:
1. **v1.1 (first month): daily reward + a weekly Cold Case streak event** — the cheapest proven ARPDAU/retention lift, and our cold-case loop is already the scaffold for it.
2. **v1.2: evidence board becomes an active meta** — pins as collectibles, board completion rewards per episode; consider a light "restore Ally's office" decoration loop drawing on the merge board's output.
3. **Content cadence commitment:** Episode 2 within 6–8 weeks of launch (the script already exists); episodes are our "events" until real liveops exist.
4. **Second/third rewarded placements** (cold-case reroll, board slot) once brand trust is established.

## Part 4 — Actions inside the current plan (no scope change)

- **Sprint 9 ASO is now first-class work**: keyword field ("true crime merge," "detective story game," "mystery merge"), screenshots that lead with story/portraits not board, and the subtitle carrying the podcast hook.
- **VO does double duty**: cut 3–4 of Ally's podcast nodes as vertical video clips with captions — that's the TikTok/Shorts pipeline seeded at zero extra production cost.
- **Beta instrumentation already ships**: the Firebase funnel gives D1/D7 and story-completion out of the box; add the genre benchmarks to the beta review checklist.
- **Do not add features to v1.** The buffer is spoken for (Episode 1 + portraits/VO/choices), and everything in Part 3 works better with live player data anyway.
