# Episode 1 Pilot Spec — the job description
*Every pitch is judged against this. Player experience is the spine. v1 · 2026-07-10*

## What the pilot must do (in priority order)

1. **Hook inside 60 seconds.** The tip lead's first two nodes are the game's real cold open — they play during/right after the FTUE. A brand-new player with zero context must finish node 2 needing to know what happened. The hook is *lines*, not premise.
2. **Make the jeopardy present-tense.** Someone must be in danger *now*, or Ally must come into danger *during* the episode. No purely historical harm, no purely institutional villains. The antagonist is a person with a face (or a terrifying absence where a face should be).
3. **Establish Ally's wound without spending it.** The player learns: father dead, suspicious, unfinished business, the locket, why she holds a microphone at the dark. The season network (Ferryman/Hart/Voss) stays *invisible* — except the whisper (below).
4. **Teach the loop under the story.** Leads 1–3 are most players' entire first session. Each must end on a pull to the next. The merge requirements must feel like *doing the investigation* (gathering forensic tools = processing a scene), never like a toll booth.
5. **Complete the case with emotional honesty.** A victim who is a person; a keeper character who carried the case and gets an on-page resolution beat; truth delivered even where justice can't be.
6. **Plant the whisper.** The final act surfaces ONE unexplained detail that means nothing to Ally yet and everything to the player who continues: **a coin, somewhere it shouldn't be.** No explanation, no emphasis from Ally beyond noticing. Episode 2 (The Ferryman) detonates it.
7. **Convert.** The case-close podcast must leave the player wanting Episode 2 more than they wanted Episode 1 — via the whisper plus the feeling that Ally has crossed a line she can't uncross (someone now knows her name).

## Relaxed constraints (the greenlit package — write FOR these)

- **Portraits:** episode witnesses get faces and may hold their own dialogue nodes with the full 7-emotion set. Budget: up to 4 new portrait characters per episode (art is AI-generated per character sheet). Ally and Gerald remain the anchors.
- **One branch point:** exactly one choice moment per episode, placed in Phase 2, with two options that differ in *approach* (compassion vs. pressure, publish vs. protect) — both paths reach the same case resolution but change one scene's content and one line in the finale. No branching plot topology.
- **VO:** all Podcast-type leads (L1, L5, L12) are voiced by Ally. Write those nodes for the ear — rhythm, breath, no visual crutches.

## Unchanged constraints

12 leads / 2 phases / gates per the writers' prompt v2 · types (Discuss = Gerald/Helen only) · titles ≤32 chars, subtitles ≤140 · 3–5 nodes per lead, ~44–48 total · emotions rationed (`angry` once, earned) · lines ≤45 words · 12+ rating · canon-first rule · anti-patterns list · requirement families: forensic tools, fingerprint evidence, harbour/bar, diner food (garage/press exist but are art-gated — avoid as primary).

## Season duties transferred from Ghost Student

The old Ep1 introduced Voss Bio and Gerald's lockbox reveal. In the new season ladder those move later. The new pilot carries only: (a) the coin whisper, (b) the first light touch of Thomas (his absence, his files existing, not their contents), (c) one recurring-cast introduction beyond Gerald (pick from Mo, Del Cruz, Arthur, Priya — whoever the case naturally needs first).

## First-session shape (leads 1–3, the retention window)

- L1 (Podcast/tip): the hook. Ends with Ally committing — and the player's first merge goal framed as her first move.
- L2–L3 (parallel): one *person* lead (meet the human stakes) and one *place/evidence* lead (meet the world). Each ends with a small reveal that reframes the tip.
- If a player closes the app after L3, the question in their head must be strong enough to reopen it tomorrow: regen cadence means they return with full energy — give them a cliff worth returning to.

## Pilot Doctrine (v2 addendum — every pitch must engineer these, in roughly this order)

1. **Dramatic question in ≤2 nodes.** The cold open poses a question the player cannot leave.
2. **Ally's warmth beat** — a concrete act of kindness that costs her something (save the cat), performed not narrated, inside L1–L2.
3. **Ally's competence beat** — she does something investigatively brilliant on-page, inside L1–L2.
4. **The grievable victim** — built from ONE concrete humanizing detail, not biography. (Attachment is the pilot's OUTPUT, never its input: no pitch may rely on pre-existing love for any character.)
5. **Every recurring character gets an engineered entrance** — Gerald's first scene must itself create the attachment any later scene spends.
6. **Jeopardy reaches Ally personally by the midpoint.**
7. **Vulnerability beat** — one private crack in Ally's competence (the locket is purpose-built), placed before the climax.
8. **Guided tour** — the case's first three leads teach the series engine under the story.
9. **Effort engine** — the keeper visibly benefits from the player's per-lead work (interactive attachment: players love who they labor for).
10. **Whiff of change + the coin** — Ally ends the pilot irreversibly altered; the whisper lands unexplained.

## Judging rubric for pitches (score each 1–5)

1. Cold-open strength (would you keep listening after node 2?)
2. Present-tense jeopardy
3. Emotional keeper (who carries the case, does their beat land?)
4. Clarity — can the premise be said in one sentence a store review would quote?
5. Canon nativeness (places, cast, motifs earn their use)
6. Whisper elegance (does the coin land without Ally understanding it?)
7. Branch-point quality (is the choice one a player will argue about?)
8. Teachability (do the first 3 leads map naturally to merge goals?)
